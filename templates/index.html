Can A Computer Tell?

Let's find out

Motivation

We are so use to reading and seen people faces that it become natural of just predict what other is thinking or feeling. But can it be done through computer vision? is it possible to have a computer to guess what the person is feeling at the time with just their facial expression? So today, our team took up this challenge and we are planning to answer it with deep learning neural network that give computer vision the ability to recognize how one feel at given moment.

Beginning of the Journey

In order to make a machine (computer) understand to know how you feel, there are a lot of thing that we have to first teach it. We need to first teach a computer to look (use webcam), then where to look (identify the target: human face) *Sometime we do caught elf lurking in the background...
A large set of human face data is necessary to feed into the program so it knows what a human face is, so Kaggle Facial Expression Recognition Challenge (FER2013) fit our need.

Feeding "Emotion Model"

In Machine Learning term, "Model" commonly known as a collection of rules for computer to process data, other may see it as the regression data and related statistics. For our project's demonstration need, we had found a well-trained keras CNN (known as "Convolutional Nerual Network") XCEPTION model from B-IT-BOTS team that has ~66% accuracy of predicting a human facial expression.
*Please also checkout of their publication for the details of how the model was trained.

Finding the "FACE"

There are plenty of different method/algorithm/model that are good at detecting what a human face is. We had found that what OpenCV Haar-Casscade Model offered work the best with not only locating where the human face in a image/video, but also very capable of reshaping/pre-processing the data source into computer best understand/digest format.

Making it "ALIVE"!

A long journey has lead us to point... We have created a web application using Python Flask that allow user to submit an image of a human face, and the computer will be able to "detect" where the face is located at the picture then crop it out. Once it is crop and pre-process into a grayscale format, then it will pass into our Keras CNN model where the computing happen!
(or call it "magic" which ever way you preferred...)
